Punjab's Dish saag

Saag is a traditional North Indian dish made from leafy greens like mustard leaves, spinach, fenugreek, or bathua. The greens are cooked, mashed, and flavored with onions, garlic, ginger, green chilies, and spices. Sometimes maize flour is added for a smooth texture. The most famous version is Sarson da Saag, usually served with Makki di Roti and topped with butter or ghee, making it a beloved Punjabi winter delicacy. Saag is not only tasty but also rich in vitamins, minerals, and iron, making it very healthy.

Ingredients

  • leaves (sarson) – main ingredient
  • Spinach (palak) – optional, for balance
  • Fenugreek leaves (methi) – optional
  • Green chilies
  • Ginger
  • Garlic
  • Onions (optional)
  • Maize flour (makki ka atta) – for thickness
  • Ghee or butter – for cooking and garnish
  • Salt – to taste
  • Spices – cumin, coriander, turmeric, red chili powder
  1. Clean and wash leaves – Wash mustard leaves, spinach, and bathua (or any greens you use) thoroughly to remove dirt.
  2. Chop the greensRoughly chop the leaves into medium pieces.
  3. the greens – In a deep pot, add the chopped leaves, some green chilies, ginger, garlic, and a little water. Let them cook on low heat until soft (about 30–40 minutes).
  4. Mash or blend– Once the leaves are soft, mash them with a wooden spoon (madhani) or blend lightly to make a coarse paste.
  5. maize flour – Mix in 1–2 tablespoons of maize flour (makki ka atta) to thicken the saag. Cook for another 10–15 minutes, stirring often.
  6. Prepare tempering tadka Garlic– Heat ghee or butter in a pan, add chopped onions, garlic, and a pinch of spices like cumin, red chili, and coriander. Fry until golden.
  7. Mix and simmer– Add this tempering to the cooked saag, mix well, and let it simmer for 5–10 minutes.
  8. Maize flour (makki ka atta)– for thickness
  9. Serve hot– Top with a little butter and enjoy with Makki di Roti (corn flour flatbread).
